Discover Taniti

Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific with an area of less than 500 square miles. Our varied terrain includes sandy and rocky beaches, a safe harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and a mountainous interior featuring a small, active volcano.

Home to an indigenous population of about 20,000, Taniti offers an authentic experience blending traditional culture with modern amenities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Taniti uses the U.S. Dollar (USD) as its official currency.

  • Many businesses also accept Euros (€) and Japanese Yen (¥)
  • Several banks offer currency exchange services
  • Most businesses accept major credit cards
  • ATMs available in Taniti City

Taniti offers options for every budget:

  • Budget ($50-100/day): Hostel, local restaurants, public buses
  • Mid-Range ($150-250/day): Hotels/B&Bs, mixed dining, rental car
  • Luxury ($300+/day): Four-star resort, fine dining, private tours

Activities: Snorkeling from $35, Volcano tours from $75, Zip-lining from $45

Shopping options include:

  • Art Galleries: Local and visiting artists in Taniti City
  • Merriton Landing: Developing area with shops and entertainment
  • Local Markets: Fresh produce, handcrafted goods, souvenirs
  • Grocery: 2 supermarkets, 2 smaller stores, 1 24-hour convenience store

Note: Many shops close on national holidays

Respect local customs:

  • Language: Younger Tanitians speak English. Little English in rural areas.
  • Holidays: Many national holidays — attractions may close
  • Alcohol: Not sold between midnight and 9:00 AM. Drinking age is 18.
  • Dress: Casual. Cover up when entering town from beach.
  • Tipping: Appreciated but not mandatory (10-15%)

Taniti is very safe. Violent crime is extremely rare.

  • Some pickpocketing reported — keep valuables secure
  • One hospital with multilingual staff and several clinics
  • Most areas safe to walk, even at night

Essential details:

  • Power: 120 volts (same as US)
  • Healthcare: Hospital and clinics available
  • Bikes: Rentals available. Helmets required by law.
  • Buses: Run 5:00 AM to 11:00 PM in Taniti City
  • Walking: Taniti City is flat and walkable

Accessibility options:

  • Lodging: Resort and several hotels have ADA-accessible rooms
  • Attractions: Yellow Leaf Bay beaches and History Museum accessible
  • Transport: Some buses accessible. Rental agencies can arrange vehicles.
  • Dining: Many restaurants are ground-level and accessible

Use the ♿ ADA Accessible filter to find options.

Taniti has tropical weather year-round:

  • Peak: December-April — dry season, best weather
  • Shoulder: May and November — fewer crowds
  • Off-Peak: June-October — occasional rain, best prices

Check holiday calendar before booking!
